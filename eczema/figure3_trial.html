Figure 3. Proposed Clinical Trial Designs to Test the Gut–Butyrate–NRF2 Hypothesis in Atopic Dermatitis
Three complementary randomized controlled trials targeting distinct nodes of the hypothesized gut–butyrate–skin barrier axis. Trial designs progress from direct supplementation (Trial 1) through multi-target natural intervention (Trial 2) to whole-diet modification (Trial 3).
Figure 3. Three complementary randomized controlled trial designs proposed to test the gut–butyrate–NRF2 hypothesis in atopic dermatitis (AD). Trial 1 (blue) is a double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT of oral sodium butyrate supplementation—the single most critical missing experiment—with comprehensive mechanistic biomarkers including epidermal NRF2 targets (HO-1, NQO1), filaggrin immunohistochemistry, and circulating Foxp3⁺ regulatory T cells. Trial 2 (green) employs a 2×2 factorial design to determine whether gut-directed therapy (prebiotic fiber + probiotic) and skin-directed therapy (topical vitamin B12 + colloidal oatmeal) have additive or synergistic effects, with 16-week follow-up to assess remission durability. Trial 3 (orange) tests whole-diet modification (high resistant starch + inulin) to evaluate the complete causal chain from dietary fiber through microbiome remodeling and butyrate production to skin barrier improvement. Together, these trials address the key evidence gaps identified in the systematic review: the absence of direct butyrate supplementation data (Trial 1), the lack of multi-target combination approaches (Trial 2), and the need to validate food-based interventions targeting the gut–skin axis (Trial 3). All trials are designed for CONSORT-compliant reporting and pre-registration. Abbreviations: AD, atopic dermatitis; CDLQI, Children's Dermatology Life Quality Index; FLG, filaggrin gene; GC-MS, gas chromatography–mass spectrometry; HO-1, heme oxygenase-1; IHC, immunohistochemistry; ITT, intention-to-treat; NQO1, NAD(P)H quinone dehydrogenase 1; NRF2, nuclear factor erythroid 2–related factor 2; RCT, randomized controlled trial; RS, resistant starch; SCFA, short-chain fatty acid; SCORAD, Scoring Atopic Dermatitis; TEWL, transepidermal water loss; TID, three times daily; Tregs, regulatory T cells.
